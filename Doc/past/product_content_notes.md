# Product Content Notes

## Best Product Frame

This should be sold as a practical emotional relief product, not a psychology lecture.

Strongest frame:

> The copy-paste boundary kit for women who are tired of over-giving.

Secondary frame:

> Scripts for saying no without guilt, overexplaining, or sounding rude.

## What The User Is Really Buying

The user is not only buying scripts. She is buying:

- Relief from decision stress.
- Permission to protect herself.
- Words when she freezes.
- A way to stay kind without self-abandoning.
- Confidence during emotionally loaded conversations.
- A repeatable system for money, work, family, friends, and home.

## Premium Guide Experience

The paid document should feel like a practical toolkit, not a long ebook.

Front-load the purchase confidence:

- A buyer should see what to do in the first 10 minutes.
- A buyer should see exact situations where the guide applies.
- A buyer should see screenshot-worthy cards she can save to her phone.
- A buyer should understand she is buying ready words, emotional relief, and a repeatable system.

Avoid promising a specific conversion rate. The strongest honest claim is:

> Built to help you get useful words before your next hard conversation.

## Product Modules

### Module 1: Money Without Guilt

Helps users decline lending money, split costs fairly, handle expensive plans, and respond to repeated pressure.

### Module 2: Social Energy Protection

Helps users decline invitations, cancel plans, avoid overexplaining, and suggest alternatives when they genuinely want connection.

### Module 3: Work Pushback

Helps users handle extra tasks, unclear priorities, after-hours messages, coworker handoffs, and boss scope-creep.

### Module 4: Household Delegation

Helps users shift from "reminding and managing" to full ownership by partners or housemates.

### Module 5: Family And Guilt

Helps users respond to guilt, obligation, last-minute requests, and old family patterns without escalating.

### Module 6: Emotional Labor

Helps users stop becoming the always-available listener, mood regulator, or crisis support person.

### Module 7: Pushback Library

Helps users stay steady when someone argues, guilt-trips, sulks, or keeps negotiating.

## Messaging Rules

- Use "women" instead of "girls" unless speaking specifically about minors.
- Avoid manipulative words like "hack," "trick," or "socially engineered" in customer-facing copy.
- Avoid claiming scripts are "proven to work" unless backed by a specific study or test.
- Avoid blaming the user for over-giving.
- Avoid portraying boundaries as selfishness.
- Keep the tone warm, practical, and non-shaming.
- Make the scripts short enough to copy into a real text message.
